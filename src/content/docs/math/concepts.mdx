## Homogeneity

### Algebratic Homogeneity

A function $ f(x, y, ...) $ is homogeneous of degree $ k $ if $ f(t x, t y, ...) = t^k f(x, y, ...) $. For example,

$$
    f(x, y, z) = (x + y) / z
$$

is homogeneous of degree 0 because:

$$
    (tx + ty) / (tz) = (t(x + y)) / (tz) = t^0 (x + y) / z
$$

### Homogeneous polynomials

Loosely speaking, polynomials of $ x, y, ... $ are homogeneous if powers of $ x, y, ... $ add up to the same total degree. For example,

$$
    x^2 + 3xy - 2y^2
$$

is homogeneous because $ 2 = 1 + 1 = 2 $. Homogeneous polynomials are always homogeneous in degree $ k $ which is equal to the polynomial's degree (its largest power). For example,

$$
    2x^3 + x^2 y + 8y^3
$$

is homogeneous of degree $ 3 $:

$$

## Rational functions

Rational functions are functions that are quotients of two polynomials. For example, the function

$$
    y = (P(x)) / (Q(x))
$$

where $ P(x), Q(x) $ are polynomials, is a rational function.
